The Department of Veterans Affairs is committed to increasing access to VA healthcare for its rural Veterans by providing new outpatient facilities in areas where Veterans currently travel long distances for their care. The clinic is staffed by VA personnel. The VA Newport Outpatient clinic first opened its doors to northern Vermont Veterans on the 12th of August, 2010 at the North Country Hospital location. The 1734 Crawford Farm Road clinic space serves the primary health care needs of eligible veterans in the Newport, Vermont area, while mental health services will continue to be available at Northeast Kingdom Human Services on 154 Duchess Avenue in Newport.

SERVICES:

Services provided and arranged for through the Clinic

  • Primary Care
  • Laboratory Tests
  • Preventive services such as annual flu and pneumonia shots
  • Electrocardiograms (EKG)
  • X-rays
  • Medications

ADVANTAGES:

  • Veterans will have one primary care doctor to coordinate care on an ongoing basis.
  • Veterans can receive primary medical care closer to home and don’t have to travel to the White River Junction VA Medical Center.
  • Veterans will still be eligible for specialty outpatient care, inpatient care and all other entitlements through the White River Junction VA Medical Center.
